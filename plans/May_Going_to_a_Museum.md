# ESL Lesson Plan: Going to a Museum
**Student:** Hsuan (A2-B1) | **Lesson Type:** Type A (Article/Topic-Based)

## 🎯 Expected Goals
1. **Practical Vocabulary & Prepositions:** Learn and apply essential museum vocabulary while naturally practicing prepositions of place (e.g., *on* the second floor, *in* the museum) found in the dialogue.
2. **Tense Switching Practice:** Actively practice the past tense when recalling past museum visits, and present tense when discussing general preferences.

## ⏱️ Pacing & Rhythm (50 mins total)
*   **0-10m (Slides 1-2):** Greeting, Small Talk, and reviewing any previous corrections (check in on her pregnancy/maternity leave preparations).
*   **10-20m (Slide 3):** Topic introduction via Warm-up questions. Focus on getting her to use the past tense for question 2.
*   **20-35m (Slide 4 & Reading):** Vocabulary review and Dialogue Reading. Roleplay the Tourist and Attendant to practice pronunciation and intonation.
*   **35-45m (Slide 5 Prep):** Guided speaking practice preparing for the homework task. Brainstorm ideas together.
*   **45-50m:** Wrap-up, feedback, and homework assignment.

## 🔑 Customization Strategy
Because Hsuan struggles with switching between present and past tense, the warm-up questions are specifically mixed (Q1 present, Q2 past) to force a conscious switch. The vocabulary definitions from the Elli worksheet have been slightly simplified to ensure they are accessible for an A2-B1 learner, keeping the focus on speaking rather than reading complex definitions. The homework task directly challenges her to tell a cohesive story in the past tense.

### Slide 4
```text
admission fee   | the cost to enter a building or special event
donation        | money that you volunteer to give 
fossil          | remains of something from long ago found inside rock
brochure        | printed information about a place or event
exhibit         | a single item or small group of items on display (展品)
exhibition      | a large, organized event showing many exhibits together (展覽)
exhibitor       | a person who is displaying something 
wander          | to walk around casually without a plan
gift shop       | a store that sells souvenirs or gifts
permit          | to allow
flash           | a bright light that goes off on a camera

*Types of Museums & Exhibitions:*
- Art Exhibition (藝術展): Paintings, sculptures, and modern art.
- Historical Exhibition (歷史展): Artifacts and stories from the past.
- Science Exhibition (科學展): Space, biology, and interactive experiments.
- Natural History Exhibition (自然史展): Dinosaur fossils, animals, and nature.
- Photography Exhibition (攝影展): Collections of photographs.
```
