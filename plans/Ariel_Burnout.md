# 📝 ESL Lesson Plan: Burnout & Setting Boundaries
**Student:** Ariel
**Level:** B1
**Lesson Type:** Type A (Article/Topic-Based)

## 🎯 Expected Goals
1. **Fluency & Detail:** Encourage Ariel to express her thoughts on workplace stress and boundaries using full sentences and personal examples.
2. **Vocabulary Reinforcement:** Solidify her understanding of the burnout-related vocabulary introduced in the previous lesson and apply it in speaking.
3. **Targeted Weakness Practice:** Provide natural opportunities to use past tense correctly (when discussing past stressful experiences) and distinguish between "went home" / "got home".

## ⏱️ Pacing & Rhythm (50 mins total)
* **0-10m (Small Talk):** Greeting, weekend review, and quickly revisiting the "went home vs got home" distinction.
* **10-20m (Slide 1):** Topic introduction via Warm-up questions relating to her daily work life.
* **20-35m (Slides 2 & 3):** Vocabulary review and comprehension check of the Burnout article. (Since she started this last time, focus on reading the rest and checking understanding).
* **35-45m (Slide 4):** Post-discussion. Focus heavily on getting Ariel to share her own experiences and opinions about setting boundaries.
* **45-50m (Slide 5):** Wrap-up, feedback, and homework assignment.

## 🔑 Customization Strategy
* **Personalized Context:** Questions are tailored to her dual roles (project engineer and tutor) to make the burnout topic highly relevant.
* **Recycling Vocabulary:** Words marked as "練習中" from the last session (exhaustion, prolonged, resentfulness) are heavily featured.
* **Targeting Weaknesses:** Question 2 in the Post-Discussion is specifically designed to elicit the use of "when I get home" to practice her known weak point.

### Slide 2: Vocabulary (Part 1)
```text
Word | Definition
Exhaustion (n.) | a state of extreme physical or mental tiredness
Prolonged (adj.) | continuing for a long time
Constant (adj.) | happening all the time or repeatedly
Resentfulness (n.) | a feeling of anger about an unfair situation
Doubt (v.) | to feel uncertain about something or yourself
```

### Slide 2: Vocabulary (Part 2)
```text
Word | Definition
Insomnia (n.) | difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep
Prioritize (v.) | to treat something as more important than other things
Boundaries (n.) | limits that you set to protect your time and energy
Overextend (v.) | to try to do more than you have the time or energy for
Indicator (n.) | a sign that shows you what is happening
```

### Slide 3: Comprehension Questions
```text
1. According to the article, what characterizes the condition of burnout?
A) A temporary feeling of physical tiredness.
B) A combination of mental, physical, and emotional exhaustion.
C) A sudden decrease in workplace productivity.
D) A cognitive difficulty in retaining new information.
Answer: B

2. What happens to the brain during an extended "fight or flight" response?
A) It gains better access to logic and decision-making.
B) It loses access to frontal lobe functions.
C) The heart rate decreases to save energy.
D) It becomes more focused on long-term planning.
Answer: B

3. What is indicated about the physical effects of burnout?
A) It primarily causes temporary joint pain.
B) It leads to an increase in cognitive ability.
C) It can result in conditions such as insomnia and high blood pressure.
D) It decreases the likelihood of developing diabetes.
Answer: C

4. What does the author suggest as a short-term method to lower cortisol levels?
A) Taking a brief nap at the desk.
B) Engaging in a short period of physical exercise.
C) Reviewing emails to prioritize tasks.
D) Consuming a caffeinated beverage.
Answer: B
```

### Slide 4: Post-discussion Questions
```text
1. The article mentions setting boundaries at work. As an engineer and a tutor, is it easy or difficult for you to say "no" to extra tasks?
2. How do your cats (especially the emotional one!) help you relieve stress when you get home from work?
3. The author Anne Lamott says: "Almost everything will work again if you unplug it for a few minutes, including you." Do you agree? How do you usually "unplug"?
```

### Slide 5: Homework
```text
Preventing Burnout: My Strategy

Task: Describe your personal strategy for avoiding burnout and managing stress.

Guiding Questions:
• What are the first indicators that tell you you're doing too much?
• What boundaries do you try to set at your factory job or with your tutoring?
• How do you plan to "unplug" during your next days off?
```
