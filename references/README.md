## Style Patterns by Level

### A2 (Below B1)

#### Small Talk (Slide 2)
- Scaffolded with sentence pattern + fill-in-the-blank phrases with illustrations
- Pattern: "What did you do this week? This week, I + past verb + (object/place)"
- Options underneath images: "spent time with ___", "caught up with ___", "watched ___", "went to a/the ___"
- Same layout used across both A2 examples

#### Grammar Practice Slide (Slide 2b, A2 only)
- Appears after small talk, before the main content
- A single targeted grammar question with a sentence starter (e.g., "What were you doing this afternoon? I was ……")
- Reinforces the grammar point being practiced in the lesson

#### Vocabulary (Slide 4)
- **Max 4 words per slide** (not 5)
- Split across two slides if more than 4 words (e.g., Bad Bunny: 4+4 = 8 total)
- Simpler, shorter definitions than B1
- Two-column table: Word | Definition

#### Homework (Slide 5)
- Uses **sentence patterns** (not guiding questions)
- Structured with numbered sections (1. Introduction, 2. Details, 3. Tools and Gears)
- Each section has guiding Qs + sentence starters

#### Type B: Grammar-Focused Lessons (A2 only so far)
- **Homework Review slides:** Previous homework broken into 2-3 numbered sub-slides (e.g., "I went camping last weekend" → 1. Introduction, 2. Activities, 3. Feelings). Each has a bold guiding Q + 3-4 sentence patterns.
- **Grammar Teaching slides (build-up approach):**
  - Slide G1: Question using target grammar + 3 example answers with grammar highlighted in distinct color
  - Slide G2: Same content faded/greyed, concept-check Q added below (e.g., "Is this happening now or every day?")
  - Slide G3: Grammar label revealed in English + Chinese (e.g., "Present Progressive 現在進行式")
  - Slide G4: Free practice question, no scaffolding

### B1

#### Small Talk (Slide 2)
- Open-ended feelings check-in: "How are you feeling today? I feel / am ___."
- Categorized into Positive, Neutral, Negative columns
- No sentence scaffolding — student speaks freely

#### Warm-up Questions (Slide 3)
- 3 numbered discussion questions introducing the topic
- Row of 4-5 topic-related images/icons below
- 3 bullet follow-up questions referencing the images (e.g., "Which one is the most common phobia?")

#### Vocabulary (Slide 4)
- **Max 5 words per slide**
- Split across two slides if more than 5 words
- Definitions slightly more detailed than A2 but still simple English
- Two-column table: Word | Definition

#### Homework (Slide 5)
- Uses **guiding questions** (not sentence patterns)
- Topic framed as a question/opinion prompt (e.g., 📚: "Notifications — Helpful or Stressful?")
- Task instruction + 4-5 open-ended guiding questions

### B2-C1

#### Small Talk (Slide 2) — Multi-slide "Weekly Life Update"
- **Slide 2a: Starting Phrases** — natural ways to open about the week. Key collocations highlighted in red (e.g., "Last week was **pretty**… busy/quiet/normal", "I had a **fairly** busy week", "It went by quickly").
- **Slide 2b: Work & weekday life** — phrases for work ("I worked overtime", "It was a **typical** workweek", "I finished work late a few days").
- **Slide 2c: Weekend Phrases** — phrases for weekends ("I took it easy", "I went out for a bit", "I met up with friends").
- **Slide 2d: Follow-up Questions** — how to continue the conversation ("How about you?", "Did you do anything fun?", "Was your week busy?", "How was your weekend?").
- No scaffolding blanks — provides natural phrases for conversational fluency.

## Visual Style (consistent across all levels)
- **Template:** Yellow/black Canva frame — rounded yellow rectangles in top corners, black bracket-style borders on sides
- **Font:** Bold sans-serif for headings, regular for body
- **Vocabulary table:** Yellow header row, alternating white rows with thin orange/yellow borders
- **Color highlights:** Red for key collocations (B2-C1), green/teal for grammar targets (A2), bold black for everything else
